Accelerating Digital Transformation in a Global Enterprise

Multinational Technology & Manufacturing

Customer Profile – Background & Industry

A multinational organization in the technology and manufacturing sector embarked on a large-scale modernization program, migrating core platforms and embedding agile practices across thousands of staff.

Challenges Faced

• Siloed departments with competing priorities

• Slow delivery cycles and frequent bottlenecks

• Change fatigue and limited buy-in from business and technology leaders

Our Approach (Step by Step)

Discovery & Alignment – Clarify business outcomes; align executives around value, scope, and success metrics (OKRs/KPIs).

Current-State Mapping – Map value streams, dependencies, and bottlenecks across product, platform, data, and compliance.

Operating Model Design – Define a framework-agnostic agile operating model (cadences, roles, governance) tailored to context.

Pilot & Prove – Launch 2–3 cross-functional pilot teams to validate practices, tooling, and delivery cadence.

Scale & Synchronize – Introduce lightweight portfolio governance, predictability measures, and dependency management.

Capability Uplift – Coach leaders and teams; build internal communities of practice; establish playbooks.

Measure & Iterate – Use leading/lagging indicators to refine ways of working; sunset practices that don't add value.

Solutions Implemented

• Introduced a context-specific agile delivery model grounded in design thinking and systems thinking

• Built cross-functional value-stream teams and executive steering ceremonies

• Implemented OKRs/KPIs and dashboards for transparency

Measurable Results:

• Faster delivery cadence and predictability

• Improved stakeholder alignment

• A sustainable culture of agility

Driving Agile Compliance in Financial Services

Major Financial Services Organization

Customer Profile – Background & Industry

A major financial services organization faced challenging regulatory deadlines while modernizing its digital estate.

Challenges Faced

• Complex compliance requirements with fixed timelines

• Traditional delivery approaches limiting responsiveness

• Limited visibility into release performance and quality

Our Approach (Step by Step)

Regulatory Decomposition – Translate regulations into clear, testable requirements and acceptance criteria.

Risk-Prioritized Backlog – Build an integrated compliance+product backlog, prioritized by business risk and value.

Dual-Track Delivery – Run discovery (policy, controls, data lineage) alongside delivery (build, test, release).

Controls by Design – Embed controls and auditability into pipelines (traceability, segregation of duties, evidence).

Quality & Readiness Gates – Establish definition of ready/done with compliance checkpoints and automated reporting.

Release Orchestration – Coordinate change, testing, and cutovers with clear go/no-go criteria and rollback plans.

Transparency & Governance – Provide real-time dashboards for executives; run outcome-based steering.

Solutions Implemented

• Blended agile delivery with regulatory rigor across cross-functional teams

• Implemented real-time dashboards for flow, risk, and compliance evidence

• Orchestrated testing and releases to meet fixed deadlines without sacrificing quality

Measurable Results:

• Compliance objectives met on time while sustaining delivery velocity

• Faster, data-driven decision-making and improved audit readiness

• More predictable releases and strengthened stakeholder trust

Innovating in Defence & Transport: Driverless Trains

Defence & Transport Systems Supplier

Customer Profile – Background & Industry

A defence and transport systems supplier was building driverless train control and monitoring systems for a major project, operating in a high-safety, regulated industry.

Challenges Faced

• Extremely complex safety-critical requirements with no room for error

• Hardware, software, and systems engineering teams working in silos

• Long integration cycles that delayed testing and slowed progress

Our Approach (Step by Step)

Requirements Harmonisation – Brought software, hardware, and systems teams together to jointly refine safety-critical user stories.

Agile Pilot Teams – Established small cross-functional squads integrating system engineers, testers, and developers.

Incremental Builds – Shifted from long-cycle releases to short, iterative increments with frequent integration.

Continuous Testing – Embedded automated testing in simulation environments to validate driverless controls early.

Stakeholder Reviews – Ran frequent demos with project sponsors, engineers, and safety authorities to maintain transparency.

Culture Change – Trained leaders and engineers to adopt collaborative, feedback-driven practices.

Scaling – Expanded practices across all delivery streams once pilots proved value.

Solutions Implemented

• Introduced agile and iterative engineering practices in a traditionally waterfall, compliance-heavy environment

• Unified siloed teams to co-deliver functionality in shorter cycles

• Brought transparency through continuous demos and inspection with safety stakeholders

Measurable Results:

• Integration time reduced significantly, leading to earlier discovery of safety-critical issues

• Faster cycle of feedback from engineers and safety authorities improved confidence in delivery

• Stronger culture of collaboration across engineering disciplines
